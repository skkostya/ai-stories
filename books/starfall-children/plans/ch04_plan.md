# Chapter Plan

## Goal

This chapter must widen the novel from personal anomaly into unstable pattern recognition through Marcus. It should show that his visions are not just random flashes of fear but fractured contact with possible futures, and that every branch points back to the same hidden source of the call.

## Key Events

- Marcus starts suffering repeated slips in perception where moments arrive out of order and ordinary life becomes layered with near-futures.
- At school and in the city he realizes the visions are not abstract symbols but actionable fragments that sometimes let him anticipate danger at a cost.
- A public near-accident forces Marcus to act on incomplete foresight, proving that possibility can be changed but never cleanly understood.
- Later, Marcus deliberately tests the pattern and sees that wildly different futures still converge on the same buried origin point connected to the red signal from the starfall.
- The chapter ends with Marcus choosing not just to endure the visions, but to begin tracking them as evidence.

## Character Focus

- Marcus Reed: moves from anxious detachment and ironic avoidance toward the first active decision to engage with what he sees.
- Marcus's parents: should read as caring but ordinary and limited, framing his isolation through disbelief rather than cruelty.

## Emotional Beats

- Unease from time arriving in the wrong order.
- Irritated self-protection as Marcus hides symptoms behind sarcasm.
- Acute panic during the public branching event.
- Grim clarity when repeated futures reveal one common source.
- Quiet resolve at the end when Marcus chooses to record rather than deny.

## Setup For Future

- Reinforce that Marcus sees possibilities, not certainty.
- Plant that other changed children exist inside the same larger pattern even before Marcus meets them.
- Tie the red signal and the call to a physical place or structure without fully revealing it.
- Show that using Marcus's ability causes physical strain and social risk.

## Constraints

- Keep the chapter in close third person around Marcus only.
- Do not make foresight precise, convenient, or superhero-like.
- Let ordinary environments carry the threat so the scale expands without losing grounding.
- Preserve ambiguity: Marcus can identify convergence, but not full meaning.
