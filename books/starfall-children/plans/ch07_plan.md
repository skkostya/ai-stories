# Chapter Plan

## Goal

This chapter must turn the phenomenon into open pursuit. It should show that state and corporate systems are independently converging on the changed children, make official protection feel indistinguishable from captivity, and push the main cast toward flight without fully assembling them yet.

## Key Events

- Nora notices that ordinary school and civic systems have stopped behaving like neutral background infrastructure and are now quietly profiling her movements, devices, and access patterns.
- Adrian attempts to reach one of the affected children through off-network means after realizing archival traces are being actively harvested by outside actors.
- Elias and Elena face the first direct contact from an official containment-oriented team whose language stays polite while making clear that Elias is no longer being treated as a normal teenager.
- Marcus experiences a future-splinter in which "protective custody" becomes a corridor toward erasure of choice, giving him his clearest warning yet that passivity will hand the decision to adults.
- Clara and her grandmother feel pressure from a softer, more ambiguous approach: concern, offers of help, and the frightening sense that strangers already know too much about their home life.
- A corporate move linked to Victor Soren overlaps with state pressure, confirming that more than one system is hunting the same children for different reasons.
- The chapter ends with the protagonists, still apart, each crossing the same internal threshold: staying put is no longer safety.

## Character Focus

- Nora Hale: shifts from guarded private observation to the first reluctant acceptance that secrecy alone will not protect her.
- Elias Ward: moves from fear of himself toward fear of what institutions will make of him.
- Marcus Reed: shifts from ironic witness to someone forced to treat his visions as actionable warnings.
- Clara Voss: feels the loss of ordinary domestic safety as the outside world starts pressing into the home.
- Dr. Adrian Vale: moves from pattern recognition to practical intervention, choosing risk over procedure.

## Emotional Beats

- Quiet unease as routine systems become invasive.
- Rising pressure through polite but unmistakable encirclement.
- Shock when separate lives all reveal the same pattern of pursuit.
- Moral dread as "care" and "containment" start using the same language.
- Hardening resolve as the children and Adrian realize that waiting will mean surrendering agency.

## Setup For Future

- Prepare Adrian to become the first adult point of contact who can help the children move rather than merely warn them.
- Establish both Miriam Kessler's state apparatus and Victor Soren's corporate apparatus as credible but distinct threats with different logic.
- Plant the first practical pathways that will let the cast converge in Chapter 08 without making that convergence happen too early here.
- Reinforce that the world is normalizing, classifying, and quietly absorbing the anomaly faster than the children can understand it.

## Constraints

- Keep the chapter grounded in scene-level pressure rather than broad exposition about politics or conspiracy.
- Preserve ambiguity around exact institutional knowledge: adults have enough data to hunt, not enough to truly understand the contact.
- Do not gather the full team in person yet; this chapter should end by forcing movement, not by resolving it.
- Keep state actors and corporate actors differentiated in tone, method, and moral rationale.
- Any ability use should carry cost, instability, or exposure rather than reading as empowerment.
