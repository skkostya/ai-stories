# Chapter Plan

## Goal

This chapter must show the first truly dangerous consequence of the starfall through Elias. It should turn his earlier bodily wrongness into an uncontrolled gravity event, make the cost of his ability physical and moral, and show that adult fear now reacts faster than care.

## Key Events

- In the days after the meteor shower, Elias experiences intermittent episodes of pressure, disorientation, and abnormal heaviness around ordinary objects.
- Elena notices the change and pushes him to be honest, but Elias keeps hiding the severity because he fears becoming a problem to solve.
- A tense incident in a grounded everyday setting escalates when Elias loses control under stress and nearly kills or crushes someone without intending to.
- The immediate adult reaction is not explanation but fear, restraint, and the instinct to contain him.
- The chapter ends with Elias understanding that his strength is not emerging as power, but as danger that can turn him into exactly what he fears.

## Character Focus

- Elias Ward: moves from denial and defensive anger into raw fear of himself; he wants control, but his first major act proves that wanting it is not enough.
- Elena Ward: remains caring and practical, but this chapter should let fear enter her perception without making her cruel.

## Emotional Beats

- Irritation and denial as Elias tries to live normally.
- Bodily unease as gravity-related effects worsen in small private ways.
- Sudden panic during the uncontrolled surge.
- Shame and horror after the damage.
- Cold emotional fallout when adults look at Elias differently.

## Setup For Future

- Confirm that Elias's ability has a physical cost and can spread harm to bystanders.
- Plant that family loyalty may conflict with public safety and outside authority.
- Strengthen Elena as an adult ally who may still choose protection over transparency.
- Show the first local social fracture around a changed child without yet triggering full institutional pursuit.
- Leave Elias with a concrete fear that he could become a weapon in someone else's hands.

## Constraints

- Keep the chapter in close third person around Elias only.
- The incident must feel frightening and messy, not spectacular or triumphant.
- Adults in the scene should be credible: shocked, practical, and afraid rather than cartoonishly cruel.
- Do not make Elias understand or control gravity in any clean, superhero-like way.
- Preserve grounded science-fiction texture and avoid early exposition about the deeper alien mechanism.
