# Chapter Plan

## Goal

This chapter must narrow the story from the scale of the starfall to Nora's private aftermath. It should show that the anomaly did not end with the sky, make technology around her respond in increasingly specific ways, and push her from guarded curiosity into active fear and secret investigation.

## Key Events

- A few days after the starfall, Nora keeps returning to the glitched recording and notices that the anomaly resists normal playback or export.
- At school and in everyday spaces, nearby devices begin to flicker, desync, or display fragments tied to the same impossible symbol pattern from chapter 1.
- Nora tries to hide the problem from other people because she expects disbelief, pity, or surveillance rather than help.
- A small but undeniable physical trace survives: a captured fragment of signal, waveform, or visual strip that proves to her the pattern is real.
- By the end of the chapter, the response from the machines feels less like malfunction and more like something adapting to her attention.

## Character Focus

- Nora Hale: moves from skeptical observer to frightened participant; she still wants control, but the act of investigating already pulls her deeper into the phenomenon.
- The chapter should emphasize her habits of attention, impatience with vague reassurance, and fear of being turned into a conduit for something чужое.

## Emotional Beats

- Residual unease after the beauty of chapter 1.
- Irritation and self-doubt as Nora tries to explain away repeated glitches.
- Isolation when ordinary adults and peers remain inside the harmless public narrative.
- Focused curiosity as she begins testing the anomaly on purpose.
- Quiet dread when the pattern becomes too targeted to dismiss as random.

## Setup For Future

- Strengthen the motif that technology reacts to Nora before she fully understands why.
- Plant the idea that the signal can move across different devices and forms of noise rather than staying in one machine.
- Establish Nora's instinct to document first and trust later.
- Give her a concrete saved trace that can matter later when she seeks proof or comparison.
- Leave a hook that the signal may already know how to use existing systems to reach her.

## Constraints

- Keep the chapter in close third person around Nora only.
- Stay grounded in ordinary locations: school, transit, apartment, common devices.
- Avoid full explanation of the signal or overtly fluent alien communication.
- Make each anomaly small enough that outsiders could dismiss it, but together impossible for Nora to ignore.
- Preserve the thriller tone: pressure should come through ordinary infrastructure turning unreliable and intimate.
