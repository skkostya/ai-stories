# Chapter Plan

## Goal

This chapter must introduce Dr. Adrian Vale as the first adult who can connect the children's scattered anomalies into one deliberate pattern. It should move the story from isolated unease to structured scientific suspicion and end with Adrian recognizing that the starfall was guided rather than random.

## Key Events

- Adrian revisits overlooked observational data from the night of the starfall and notices trajectory behavior that should not exist in a natural meteor event.
- He cross-checks that data against scattered public and semi-public incident traces: a local gravity accident, recurring technical interference, and other child-linked anomalies that institutions are already starting to bury inside ordinary reporting systems.
- A small but concrete clue convinces him he is not chasing noise: one timing cluster or spatial overlap locks multiple incidents to the same fragment pattern.
- Adrian realizes the phenomenon is not just astronomical but selective, and that children are the consistent human variable.
- By the end of the chapter, he understands that someone else will soon see the same pattern, and delay will become dangerous.

## Character Focus

- Dr. Adrian Vale: shifts from exhausted private obsession to morally urgent commitment.
- The world around Adrian: skeptical, procedural, and slow enough to make his discovery feel fragile rather than triumphant.

## Emotional Beats

- Fatigue and old frustration from years of being almost right too early.
- Narrow professional focus turning into personal dread.
- A small, mistrusted flash of certainty.
- Shock when separate anomalies stop looking accidental.
- Quiet resolve as Adrian understands that knowledge now creates responsibility.

## Setup For Future

- Plant that Adrian has encountered a related unexplained signal in the past and failed to prove what he suspected.
- Establish that official and corporate systems are already collecting partial traces, even if they do not understand them yet.
- Confirm that the starfall was intentionally structured, not random.
- Position Adrian as a credible future ally who may choose protection over procedure.

## Constraints

- Keep the chapter in close third person around Adrian only.
- Ground every major inference in concrete data, physical records, or ordinary institutional traces rather than abstract exposition.
- Preserve uncertainty: Adrian can prove pattern and intent well enough to act, but not the full nature or motive of the contact.
- Keep adults varied and credible; resistance should come from bureaucracy, disbelief, and risk, not caricature.
