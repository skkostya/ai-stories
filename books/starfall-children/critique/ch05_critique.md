# Critique

## Structural Issues

- The chapter escalates cleanly from low-level wrongness to intimate contact, but the hidden medication reveal lands strongest if the grandmother's physical vulnerability is seeded a little earlier in the day.
- The school section works as social pressure, though it can carry slightly more causal weight if one beat there more clearly contributes to Clara's decision not to tell adults.

## Character Issues

- Clara's voice is consistent and distinct, but the grandmother's late warning about not answering the contact needs a little more grounding in lived fear so it feels earned rather than genre-aware.
- The Voice is appropriately patient and invasive, though one or two answers verge on being slightly too crisp for this stage of the mystery.

## Style Issues

- The imagery is strong and intimate, but a few formulations around the Voice can be made marginally stranger to preserve the unsettling tone.
- The chapter is generally well-paced; the goal should be to sharpen, not expand.

## Missed Opportunities

- A small earlier physical sign from the grandmother would make the kitchen incident feel less like coincidence and more like Clara crossing into knowledge she should not possess.
- Clara's choice to answer at the end could carry even more force if the earlier adult-world alternative feels one step closer and more concrete.

## Concrete Fixes

- Seed a brief sign of the grandmother's concealed health issue in the breakfast scene.
- Slightly sharpen the school aftermath so Clara more consciously rejects adult intervention.
- Make the grandmother's bedtime warning feel like a direct consequence of the kitchen scene, not just intuition.
- Blur one or two Voice responses so they remain clear but not neatly explanatory.

## Mistakes To Remember

- When a hidden domestic vulnerability drives a supernatural reveal, plant a small ordinary sign of it before the reveal pays off.
