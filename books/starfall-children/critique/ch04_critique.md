# Critique

## Structural Issues

- The chapter escalates well from private distortion to public danger and then to larger pattern recognition, but the repeated source under the ground should feel more explicitly connected to the same call rather than only to a recurring image.
- The evening news beat works as a social consequence, but the chapter is strongest when ordinary systems quietly absorb the anomaly; that thread can be sharpened a little more in revision.

## Character Issues

- Marcus's voice is consistent and distinct, but the glimpses of the other changed children risk becoming slightly too identifying this early. They should stay legible as converging lines without feeling like clean introductions.
- Marcus's refusal to tell his mother works emotionally, though it benefits from one more hint that his fear is not only disbelief but loss of control over what happens to him next.

## Style Issues

- The red imagery is effective, but the material trace on the notebook page arrives close to the border of "too overt" unless grounded by a little more ambiguity.
- A few lines about futures and wounds lean close to overt thematic phrasing; keeping them attached to concrete sensation preserves the grounded tone better.

## Missed Opportunities

- The crater-source vision can carry a stronger bodily sensation of the same silent pull already associated with the phenomenon, which would tie Marcus's knowledge to lived perception rather than abstract deduction.
- The trace left in the present can do more double work if it remains half-explainable as ordinary grime and half-impossible as a vision residue.

## Concrete Fixes

- Add a sensory bridge in the convergence vision so Marcus recognizes the place as the source of the same call, even if he cannot define it.
- Slightly blur the future images of the other children so they feel like partial recognition rather than full introduction.
- Reframe the red dust so it could almost be dismissed as roof residue, while still feeling wrong enough to unsettle Marcus.
- Make Marcus's fear of adult intervention a little more concrete in the home scene.

## Mistakes To Remember

- When a chapter introduces a major convergence point, tie the revelation to bodily perception or lived consequence, not only to explanatory thought.
