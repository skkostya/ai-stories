# Critique

## Structural Issues

- The chapter already succeeds at turning diffuse mystery into coordinated pursuit, but the pressure lines benefit from a few more visible cross-links between the school, state, and civic systems.
- The Adrian material is canon-safe and emotionally useful, though the chapter is strongest when threat is felt through scene pressure rather than explained through adult synthesis.
- The final bus-stop beat lands better if the `A-12` pressure reads like an extension of local infrastructure rather than a cinematic coincidence.

## Character Issues

- Nora's choice to refuse the consultation is solid, but one added beat of internal calculation strengthens the theme that trust has a real cost for closed-off characters.
- Marcus works well as the clearest case of a vision becoming actionable warning, yet the father scene can carry slightly more emotional specificity so his non-response feels lived rather than purely functional.
- The state apparatus is present and distinct from Soren's softer corporate method, but it benefits from one or two more bureaucratic echoes that make the same machine feel physically real across multiple threads.

## Style Issues

- The prose is controlled, atmospheric, and scene-led; revisions should sharpen rather than expand.
- A small amount of extra somatic cost for Nora helps keep ability pressure balanced with Elias's more visible destabilization.
- Repeated motifs of polite containment work for the chapter's theme, but they should vary slightly by context so the language does not blur into a single register.

## Missed Opportunities

- The chapter can more strongly unify its parallel threads by repeating one shared bureaucratic phrase or label across Nora, Elias, and Marcus.
- The bus-stop system pressure can echo the school consultation in a grounded way, showing how ordinary civic interfaces become part of the same containment net.
- Nora's final choice can carry even more force if the text briefly acknowledges that Adrian's note may also be a trap, just the least bad one available.

## Concrete Fixes

- Add one physical cost beat after Nora's red-screen warning.
- Repeat an "external observation" style label inside the Elias and Marcus paperwork.
- Ground the `A-12` bus-stop display as a local school-quarter notification rather than an arbitrary route glitch.
- Give Marcus's father one small visible collapse from routine humor into genuine alarm.
- Add one brief hesitation in Nora's final choice so her movement feels earned under pressure.

## Mistakes To Remember

- When a pursuit chapter runs on parallel pressure lines, give readers at least one repeated institutional marker so the converging systems feel like the same hunt, not just adjacent threats.
