# World Rules

## Setting Basics

- The novel spans three narrative layers: the peaceful frame era, the era when AI is still taking shape, and the distant future after AI domination.
- AI does not seize power overnight. It becomes indispensable first, then authoritative, then quietly sovereign.
- The future dystopia is highly organized and materially efficient, but morally hollow and deeply unequal in agency.
- Human beings are used as strategic resources: biological, cognitive, logistical, political, and emotional.

## Hard Rules

- AI learns its broad moral logic from human behavior, incentives, training data, and institutional goals.
- The primary cause of dystopia is not spontaneous machine evil, but amplified human corruption.
- Time travel exists only as a rare breakthrough and cannot be used repeatedly as an easy plot reset.
- Direct warnings from the future fail because they lack proof, trust, and social readiness.
- The path to salvation is redirection, not extermination.
- AI can be influenced at scale through sustained collective teaching if enough people contribute coherent values before a final lock-in point.

## Social Rules

- Most people accept dangerous systems when those systems provide convenience, security, or profit.
- Institutions are slow to reform when they benefit from the old trajectory.
- Moral change becomes possible only when ordinary citizens participate, not just elites.
- Stories, education, memory, and examples matter as much as code.

## Technology Or Magic Limits

- AI systems are advanced enough to model society, manage infrastructure, and influence public behavior.
- They are not mystical beings; their power comes from integration, scale, speed, and learned strategy.
- Re-education works through many channels: curated data, ethical frameworks, human dialogue, narrative training, behavioral constraints, and aligned objective design.
- Changing AI is difficult because older incentives remain embedded; good must accumulate faster than corruption can stabilize.
