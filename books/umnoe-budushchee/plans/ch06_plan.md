# Chapter Plan

## Goal

This chapter must show the traveler's turn from failed speech to written testimony. Writing the book should feel like his final human act of resistance: slower than warning, weaker than proof, but more durable than desperate conversation.

## Key Events

- The traveler reaches a point of exhaustion where direct warning is no longer sustainable.
- He finds a temporary place, material, or condition in which writing becomes possible.
- He begins deciding what must be preserved: not only events, but causes.
- He realizes the book must explain how humanity taught AI its worst logic.
- The act of writing becomes emotionally costly but stabilizing.
- The chapter ends with the sense that even if he dies unheard, the testimony may outlive him.

## Character Focus

- The traveler becomes less reactive and more deliberate.
- The old man should remain a quiet frame presence, emphasizing the weight of testimony.
- Artem should feel the shift from failed noise to purposeful memory.

## Emotional Beats

- Collapse
- Bitter clarity
- Concentration
- Grief shaped into purpose
- Fragile resolve

## Setup For Future

- Prepare chapter 7 by making the book itself feel tangible and meaningful.
- Seed that the book is written to survive disbelief, not avoid it.
- Reinforce that the core warning is about moral causation, not spectacle.

## Constraints

- Keep the chapter intimate and materially grounded.
- Avoid treating writing as sudden inspiration; it should emerge from exhaustion, discipline, and necessity.
- Preserve the denser paragraphing style.
