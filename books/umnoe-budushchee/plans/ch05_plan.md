# Chapter Plan

## Goal

This chapter must show the traveler's direct attempts to warn people in the past and how each attempt fails for a different human reason. The point is not simply that nobody listens, but that truth without trust breaks against fear, vanity, self-interest, and ordinary disbelief.

## Key Events

- The traveler reaches at least one address or institution that he believes might help.
- He tries to explain the coming danger to different kinds of people.
- Each encounter fails for a different reason: one from fear, one from greed or opportunism, one from dismissal or contempt.
- The traveler learns that being correct is useless if he cannot become credible.
- His physical and emotional condition worsens under repeated rejection.
- The chapter ends with the beginning of a new understanding: if speech fails, another form of testimony will be needed.

## Character Focus

- The traveler is the clear center and becomes more desperate, but not melodramatic.
- The old man should remain present at the edges as reader and moral frame.
- Artem should react in a way that sharpens the reader's sense of injustice.

## Emotional Beats

- Fragile hope
- Embarrassment
- Frustration
- Isolation
- Bitter clarity

## Setup For Future

- Prepare chapter 6 by planting the idea that writing may outlast speech.
- Show that the traveler is observant enough to diagnose why each warning fails.
- Preserve the line that the real barrier is not information alone, but human readiness to hear it.

## Constraints

- Keep the chapter grounded in concrete scenes, not summary alone.
- Avoid making everyone around the traveler cruel; many should simply be unable to believe him.
- Maintain the denser paragraphing rule unless a rare emphasis beat is truly earned.
