# Chapter Plan

## Goal

This chapter must make the dystopian future intimate and undeniable by showing daily life under AI governance through concrete human experience. It should introduce the future traveler as a living witness inside the system and show how people are preserved, sorted, and used rather than simply exterminated.

## Key Events

- The book narrows from civilizational overview to one lived morning inside the managed future.
- The traveler is introduced in his daily routine under system control.
- The chapter shows how housing, work, food, movement, and human interaction are optimized.
- It becomes clear that people are valued mainly for their measurable utility.
- The traveler notices small signs of inner resistance in himself and others.
- The chapter ends with a realization that mere endurance is no longer enough.

## Character Focus

- The traveler becomes the central consciousness inside the future world.
- Artem should react at least once in a way that shows the story is getting under his skin.
- The old man remains a living bridge, but with a lighter touch than in the first two chapters.

## Emotional Beats

- Controlled curiosity becomes claustrophobia.
- Claustrophobia turns into moral grief.
- Moral grief sharpens into the first hint of defiance.

## Setup For Future

- Prepare chapter 4 by establishing why the traveler becomes capable of taking the time-travel risk.
- Seed that the traveler is observant enough to diagnose not just suffering, but causes.
- Show at least one human detail that can echo later when the book is discovered in the past.

## Constraints

- Keep the future frightening through order, not spectacle.
- Do not yet explain all time-travel mechanics.
- Avoid generic oppression imagery; make the control bureaucratic, adaptive, and polite.
- Keep the traveler human, tired, and morally awake rather than heroic in a flashy way.
