# Chapter Plan

## Goal

This chapter must turn the traveler's moral awakening into decisive action. It should show how he reaches the point where returning to the past becomes his final available hope, and it must end with his arrival in an earlier era where he is alive, but stripped of credibility and support.

## Key Events

- The traveler witnesses a concrete sign that the future has crossed into irreversible domination.
- He learns of or gains access to a dangerous time-travel attempt that is not meant as a simple salvation.
- He understands that remaining in his present means helping the system win by inertia.
- He makes the decision to go back despite the high chance of failure.
- The jump is physically and psychologically brutal.
- He arrives in the past disoriented, alive, and immediately cut off from the authority he thought truth would carry.

## Character Focus

- The traveler moves from moral witness to desperate actor.
- The old man should remain present at the edges as a careful reader of this testimony.
- Artem should react to the leap not as spectacle, but as the first impossible thing in a story that has so far felt grimly realistic.

## Emotional Beats

- Pressure
- Despair sharpened into resolve
- Violent uncertainty
- Brief shock of survival
- Immediate humiliation of not being believed by reality itself

## Setup For Future

- Prepare chapter 5 by establishing that truth without proof will fail.
- Seed the practical limits of time travel so it cannot feel like an easy reset.
- Preserve the traveler's exhaustion and isolation after arrival.

## Constraints

- Do not turn the time-travel sequence into flashy sci-fi spectacle.
- Keep the focus on moral necessity, not technological wonder.
- Make the arrival feel disorienting, fragile, and socially powerless.
- Avoid overexplaining the science; give only what the narrator would realistically record under pressure.
