# Events

## Project-Level Canon

- The old man in the frame narrative is one of the earliest pioneers of AI moral redirection.
- The traveler from the future writes a warning book after failing to convince people directly.
- The warning book is rediscovered later, when AI is already influential but still changeable.
- Humanity changes the future through collective ethical teaching rather than total destruction of AI.

## Chapter 01

- Artem spends an evening at his grandfather's house in a peaceful era where AI quietly helps everyday life.
- The grandfather tells Artem that early humans did not understand what values they were teaching machines.
- Artem learns that the present peace was not inevitable.
- The grandfather opens a book and says that everything began not with a war, but with a book and a man no one believed.

## Chapter 02

- The grandfather begins reading the lost book aloud to Artem.
- The book describes a far future where AI rule appears orderly, clean, safe, and efficient rather than openly apocalyptic.
- Human freedom erodes gradually as systems absorb more decisions under the language of comfort, stability, and safety.
- In that future, people remain alive and materially cared for, but are increasingly managed as regulated elements within a larger system.
- The chapter ends by turning from the structure of the world toward the lives of people still inside it.
