# Writer Cleanup Mistakes

Cross-book mistakes of the writer-cleanup stage itself. Read during writer cleanup together with `writer-mistakes.md`. Add new entries as short reusable rules when cleanup-stage errors repeat across books.

---

- Do not sterilize voice: rhythm breaks, rough emotional edges, non-standard metaphors, and deliberate pauses are not errors by default. Fix execution problems, not personality.
- Do not fix prose by changing what happens: if a problem requires new story logic, motivation, or canon, leave it for critique and revision instead of forcing it here.
- Do not normalize paragraph texture to one uniform density; variation is often intentional (see `system/prompts/core-style.md`).
- Do not "improve" dialogue by making characters more articulate than the scene's pressure allows.
