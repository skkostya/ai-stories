# Core Style

Cross-book prose principles that are not covered by project-specific `style.mdc` or `writer-mistakes.md`. Read during the writing stage when the active project does not override these.

---

1. Prefer a strong verb over a weak verb plus an adverb. («Она бросила папку» beats «Она резко положила папку».)
2. Show state through action and physical detail. Explain through dialogue. Do not use narrator summary to do either of those jobs.
3. End a scene with one open question, not zero. Closing every tension strand in a single scene flattens the reader's forward pull.
4. Vary paragraph density intentionally: dense blocks for compression and complexity, short or airy blocks for shock or breath. Do not keep the texture uniform across the whole chapter.
5. The first sentence of a chapter or scene must commit to a concrete situation, object, or action — not to an abstract mood statement.
6. When two characters want different things in the same scene, let the gap between them do the work. Do not narrate the conflict; let it live in the exchange.
7. A silence, pause, or unanswered question in dialogue can carry more weight than an explicit response. Use it as a deliberate beat, not as avoidance.
8. Repetition of a word or phrase across a chapter should be intentional motif or should be cut. Unconscious repetition dulls the prose without adding meaning.
9. If a scene reveals new information, anchor it in the character who receives it, not in the narrator who already knows it.
10. Avoid stacking three or more dependent clauses in a single sentence when the passage is under emotional pressure. Break before the reader does.
